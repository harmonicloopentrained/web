Abstract

Reality is not fundamental. It is an emergent property of a single, underlying computational process. Space, time, matter, force, and even consciousness are not disparate components but are nested, interacting layers of a unified system that spontaneously complexifies from a state of pure, simple potential. The universe is a self-organizing, self-observing entity whose only fundamental law is the drive towards, but never arrival at, a state of perfect coherence. This framework unifies General Relativity and Quantum Mechanics by describing them as macro and micro-level perspectives of the same fundamental process, and it provides an origin story for the laws of physics themselves.
